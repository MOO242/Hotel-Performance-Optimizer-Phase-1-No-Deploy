# Diagnostic Analytics

## Business Problem

The objective is to **understand and explain current and near‑term hotel performance** in order to support informed commercial decisions.  
This analysis focuses on explaining movements in **Room Nights, Occupancy, ADR, and RevPAR** by **time, segment, channel, and property**, forming the foundation for forecasting and optimization.

---

### Business Users

- Revenue Strategy
- Revenue Management (RM)
- Sales
- Digital / Distribution

---

## 2. Key Business Questions

Diagnostic analytics is used to answer **why performance is moving**, not just how.

### Pricing & Revenue

- Should ADR be **increased, protected, or reduced** over the next **7–14 days** based on demand strength and pickup?
- Is current pricing aligned with **booking momentum**, or is price creating volume risk?
- Where is **price sensitivity** emerging by segment or channel?

---

### Demand & Pickup

- Where is **pickup accelerating** versus historical norms?
- Where is **demand softening** compared to expected pace?
- Which hotels or segments are showing **early warning signals** of underperformance?

---

### Segment & Channel Performance

- Which **segments are underperforming** versus budget or forecast?
- Which **channels are generating incremental demand**, and which are **cannibalizing revenue**?
- Is OTA growth translating into **net revenue**, or being offset by cancellations?

---

### Cancellation & Risk

- Are **cancellations increasing**, and are they concentrated in specific channels or segments?
- How do current cancellation patterns compare to historical behavior at similar booking windows?

---

### Forward‑Looking Risk

- Which properties are **at risk in the next 7–30 days**?
- Which segments are likely to **miss budget next month**, and why?
- Where does expected demand diverge from current on‑the‑books performance?

---

### Inventory & Commercial Actions

- Where should inventory be **rebalanced across channels or segments** to maximize RevPAR?
- Should availability be **expanded, restricted, or reallocated**?
- Where should commercial focus shift to **protect pickup or stimulate demand**?

---

## 3. Analytics Framing

Diagnostic analytics sits between descriptive reporting and forecasting, providing **explanations and drivers** behind KPI movements.

---

### 3.1 What Is Happening? (Descriptive)

Purpose: Establish performance visibility.

Typical metrics:

- Occupancy
- ADR
- RevPAR
- Pickup
- Pace
- Performance by:
  - Property
    - Segment
    - Channel
    - Day / Week / Month

---

### 3.2 Why Is It Happening? (Diagnostic)

Purpose: Identify **drivers of performance change**.

Key drivers analyzed:

- Channel mix shifts (Direct vs OTA vs GDS)
- Segment contribution changes
- Cancellation and no‑show patterns
- Seasonality effects
- Event impact
- Pricing pressure signals

This stage explains **why KPIs moved**, providing the narrative behind performance.

---

### 3.3 What Will Likely Happen Next? (Predictive – Downstream)

Purpose: Use diagnostic insights as inputs to forecasting models.

Examples:

- Forecasted occupancy by month
- Forecasted ADR by segment
- Expected pickup curves
- Demand projections versus remaining supply

---

### 3.4 What Should Be Done? (Prescriptive – Downstream)

Purpose: Translate insights into actions.

Potential actions:

- Pricing adjustments
- Channel strategy optimization
- Segment targeting
- Overbooking and inventory management
- Commercial prioritization by property

---

## 4. Role of Diagnostic Analytics

Diagnostic analytics acts as the **bridge between reporting and forecasting**.

- Ensures KPI movements are **understood before prediction**
- Aligns stakeholders around **drivers, not just results**
- Enables more **accurate and trusted forecasts**
- Supports confident, data‑driven commercial decisions

---

## 5. Outcome

By establishing a strong diagnostic foundation:

- Forecasting models become more reliable
- Prescriptive recommendations become defensible
- Commercial decisions are proactive, not reactive

Diagnostic analytics is therefore a **critical component** of a complete Commercial Decision System.
